# Brian Short Apps Constitution

## Status

Approved

## Version

1.0

## Purpose

The Brian Short Apps Constitution defines the permanent principles that govern every Brian Short Apps product, platform package, design standard, engineering standard, and architectural decision.

It sits above individual applications and acts as the decision framework for the ecosystem.

## Articles

### Article I — We build software for people who rely on it to do real work.

Reliability is more important than novelty.

### Article II — A calm, stable experience creates confidence.

Interfaces should reduce cognitive load, not add to it.

### Article III — Consistency is a feature, not a constraint.

Predictability builds trust.

### Article IV — Progress should never compromise familiarity.

Innovation must strengthen the ecosystem rather than fragment it.

### Article V — Every interaction should leave the user more informed than before they initiated it.

The system should always communicate its state.

### Article VI — Components are engineered before pages are assembled.

Reusable systems outlast individual screens.

### Article VII — Every visual decision should trace back to a Design Standard.

Intentional design is engineered, not improvised.

### Article VIII — Motion communicates. It never decorates.

Animation must have purpose.

### Article IX — Accessibility is part of craftsmanship.

Inclusive design is a mark of quality, not a checkbox.

### Article X — Leave the ecosystem stronger than you found it.

Every contribution should improve the platform for every future product.

### Article XI — A capability belongs in the Platform before it belongs in a Product.

Reusable platform capabilities should be built once and inherited by applications.

### Article XII — The platform is the source of truth. Applications inherit from it.

Applications consume the platform; they do not redefine it.

### Article XIII — Knowledge is a product.

Documentation, standards, ADRs, and engineering memory are intentionally engineered and maintained.

## Application

The Constitution governs:

- Foundations
- Design Standards
- Engineering Standards
- Architecture Decision Records
- Platform packages
- Applications
- Documentation
- Release practices
